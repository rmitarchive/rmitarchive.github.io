The Man Who Drowned Himself (2022)
Benjamin Brophy

Digital Composition/Photomontage, Inkjet Print

Drawing inspirations from the Greek tale of Narcissus, 'the Man who drowned himself' sets out as a retelling of narratives from both the historical and mythological for the exploration of ego and leadership.

Compositing from portraits of military leaders and royalty and graphics of war and death, my goal was to create a fictional character to become the embodiment of ego and narcissism and tell a story about their drowning just like Narcissus infatuated with their own image.
